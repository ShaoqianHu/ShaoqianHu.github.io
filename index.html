School of Geophysics and Geomatics, China University of Geosciences, Wuhan

I am an Associate Research Professor of Seismology at the China University of Geosciences, Wuhan. My research is focused on seismological theory and methodology innovation, as well as continental lithospheric dynamics imaging. I specialize in developing advanced techniques for ambient noise interferometry, surface wave tomography, receiver function wavefield reconstruction, and theoretical seismogram calculation. My work aims to reconstruct high-resolution, high-precision crustal and mantle structures to better understand complex tectonic systems like the Eastern Himalayan Syntaxis and the Southeastern Tibetan Plateau.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Geophysics from Saint Louis University (2017) supervised by Prof. Lupei Zhu, and my B.S. in Geophysics from the University of Science and Technology of China (2012). Before joining CUG Wuhan, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at USTC working with Prof. Huajian Yao.

Research & Achievements

Research Interests

  • Seismological Theory & Methodology: High-precision numerical computing of synthetic wavefields, differential seismograms, and green functions.
  • Ambient Noise & Surface Wave Tomography: Development of advanced dispersion extraction methods (such as frequency-Bessel transform) and direct 3D anisotropic inversion frameworks.
  • Receiver Function Wavefield Reconstruction: Reconstructing high-resolution and high-fidelity teleseismic receiver functions through innovative wavefield interpolation techniques.
  • Regional Tectonics & Geodynamics: Applying high-resolution seismic imaging to decode the crustal and lithospheric structure, rheology, and dynamics of active regions such as the Southeastern Tibetan Plateau, Eastern Himalayan Syntaxis, and the Ordos block.

Core Academic Achievements

Ambient Noise & Surface Wave Imaging Methods

Developed the multi-component frequency-Bessel (F-J) transform method, significantly improving the precision of multi-mode surface wave dispersion curve extraction. This method enables more robust constraints on shallow-to-crustal velocity structures. Furthermore, established a direct 3D radial anisotropy inversion algorithm using surface waves, bypassing traditional intermediate processing steps and overcoming inherent limitations of conventional tomography.

Receiver Function Methodology System

Proposed a receiver function wavefield reconstruction method based on stretching-and-squeezing interpolation, which substantially enhances the quality and coherence of teleseismic receiver function profiles. Developed highly efficient algorithms for computing the partial derivatives of receiver functions and regional waveforms, which significantly accelerates inversion processes while maintaining superior numerical accuracy.

Regional Tectonics & Geodynamics

Performed high-resolution crustal shear wave velocity and anisotropy imaging across active fault systems and complex tectonic zones, including the Eastern Himalayan Syntaxis, Southeastern Tibetan Plateau, and the Xiaojiang Fault Zone. These studies have successfully revealed the regional crustal rheology and geodynamic evolution processes, providing crucial physical constraints for understanding the mechanisms of regional strong earthquakes.

Theoretical Seismology Foundations

Formulated a propagation matrix algorithm for computing theoretical seismograms in spherical layered media. Investigated exact closed-form solutions for Lamb's problem under an arbitrary Poisson's ratio, derived advanced surface wave dispersion equations, and successfully resolved long-standing numerical root-loss issues in dispersion calculations.
