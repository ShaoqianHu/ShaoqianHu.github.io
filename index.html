Associate Research Professor of Seismology

School of Geophysics and Geomatics, China University of Geosciences, Wuhan

I am an Associate Research Professor of Seismology at the China University of Geosciences, Wuhan. My research focuses on seismological theory and methodology innovation, as well as continental lithospheric dynamics imaging. I develop advanced techniques for ambient noise interferometry, surface wave tomography, receiver function wavefield reconstruction, and theoretical seismogram calculation to reveal high-resolution crustal and mantle structure.

I received my Ph.D. in Geophysics from Saint Louis University in 2017 under Prof. Lupei Zhu, and my B.S. in Geophysics from the University of Science and Technology of China in 2012. Before joining CUG Wuhan, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at USTC working with Prof. Huajian Yao.

Research

Research Interests & Achievements

Thematic areas and representative methodological contributions across theory, imaging, and tectonic interpretation.

Seismological Theory & Methodology

Formulated a propagation matrix algorithm for theoretical seismograms in spherical layered media [Pub 04]; derived exact closed-form solutions for Lamb's problem [Pub 05] and advanced surface-wave counting and dispersion equations [Pub 01, Pub 06]. Developed analytic partial derivatives for both teleseismic receiver functions and regional waveforms to accelerate waveform inversion [Pub 15, Pub 14].

Ambient Noise & Surface Wave Imaging

Developed the multi-component frequency-Bessel (F-J) transform method [Pub 10], improved F-J spectrogram formulations and artifact elimination [Pub 07, Pub 02], and established a direct 3D radial anisotropy inversion algorithm that bypasses traditional intermediate steps [Pub 11]. Also investigated the azimuthal dependence of Rayleigh wave ellipticity in anisotropic media [Pub 08].

Receiver Function Methodology

Proposed a stretching-and-squeezing interpolation method for receiver function wavefield reconstruction [Pub 12] and developed efficient differential seismogram algorithms for teleseismic receiver function inversion [Pub 15]. Applied the methodologies to fault-zone imaging using dense-array receiver functions [Pub C1].

Regional Tectonics & Geodynamics

Performed high-resolution crustal radial anisotropy imaging across the Southeastern Tibetan Plateau [Pub 03] and constrained the crustal velocity structure around the Eastern Himalayan Syntaxis and the nucleation mechanism of the 2017 Mainling earthquake [Pub 13]. Revealed fault-controlled deformation in the Xiaojiang Fault Zone through ambient-noise anisotropy imaging [Pub 09].
